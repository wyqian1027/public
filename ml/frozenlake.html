Q-Learning on FrozenLake with OpenAI

The problem:

  • Description of the Game:

    Basically, the agent (the player) will be given a 2D map of a default size 4-by-4, with letter code on each location, where

    S = starting point, safe;
    F = frozen surface, safe;
    H = hole, fall to your doom;
    G = goal, where the frisbee is located.

    Your goal is to traverse from the starting point to the goal location to pick up the frisbee. For the above given map, one possible route is

    S F F F
    F H F H
    F F F H
    H F F G

    What makes the problem interesting is that, the agent are NOT always able to choose his direction. The probability of the chosen direction takes place is 33% by default. The agent might wander around back and forth for a couple of steps/episodes and finally get to the goal; or may simply missteped and fell into the hole.

    The game ends when you reach the goal or fall in a hole. You receive a reward of 1 if you reach the goal, and zero otherwise.

  • Introducing Q-learning

    Q-learning is a model-free value-based Reinforcement Learning (RF) method.

    In RF, each game forms a (finite) sequence of state(s), action(a), reward(r) chain (known as the Markov Decision Process). In theory, Q-learning defines a function \( Q(s, a) \) to represent/evaluate the discounted future reward.
