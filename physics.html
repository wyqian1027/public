  • Quantum Chromodynamical Evolution of \( \pi^+ \)(140) meson and \( \rho^+ \)(770) meson.

    With the available parton distribution functions (PDF) in our previous work, we aim to evolve it from low-momentum scale (~0.5 GeV) to the higher scale of the well-known Fermilab FNAL-E615 experimental data (~4 GeV). The next-to-next-leading order Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) equations is adopted to evolve the \( \pi^+ \) PDF. In this framework, we expect the higher scale to reveal the sea quark and gluon contributions that are missing in the current model (only valence quarks), as shown in the figure below (preliminary). Complementary calculations of moments of valence quark distributions can also be compared with other models to test the robustness of our model.

  • Stunning Wave Functions via Light Front Hamiltonian framework.

    Light mesons, such as pion and rho mesons are fundamental to our understanding of the structure of elementary particles. In this work, we adopt Light-Front coordinate and derive Hamiltonian for two-body quark antiquark interaction from first principles. It follows the natural extension of previous studies in heavier hadrons, such as Charmonium and Bottomonium. The Hamiltonian matrix is evaluated via Fortran code and diagonalized using LAPACK for high numerical precisions on state-of-the-art supercomputers. With fine computing power, several stages of truncation schemes are used. The solved eigenvalues offer us the mass spectroscopy; while the eigenvectors produce the Light-Front Wave Functions (LFWF), presented in 2D Hamoic oscillator basis.

    We produce high-quality results of physical quantities, including mass spectrum, decay constants, parton distribution functions, distribution amplitude, and form factors for the lowest 8 states in the light meson sector.   • Bound state spectroscopy for Yukawa model on the light front.

    Yukawa interaction describes the nuclear force between nucleons (fermions) mediated by pions (pseudoscalar mesons). It is directly related to understanding of deuterons, the lightest isotope. We use discretized longitudinal basis together with 2D Harmonic oscillator basis to solve the Light-Front Hamiltonian of the two-body meson exchange Yukawa interaction from ab initio principles. We obtain the mass spectroscopy and wave functions.

  • Spin Echo Phenomena in Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR). Grinnell College.

    Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is an imaging technique that renders the internal physical properties of a test object by analyzing its bulk/net nuclear magnetic signals in the presence of a magnetic field. It is believed by many to be a classical statistical problem. We supply and compare both the classical and quantum mechanical interpretations of the NMR phenomena, in particular the two-pulse and the three-pulse RF tipping experiments, with an MRI instructment. On the one hand, we use the Bloch equation to solve the spin echo amplitude. On the other hand, we derive a two-level quantum system to prove the final spin. We obtain the exact agreement between the two theories and matches the experimental results resonably well.

Astronomy

  • Comprehensive Study of Globular Cluster M13.

    We generate the star density profile of the global cluster Messier 13 (NGC 6205 or the Great Globular Cluster in Hercules), and obtain its Hertzsprung-Russel (HR) diagram with a crowded-field imaging techniques using IRAF Software. The observation is done using long exposure CCD imaging telescope at Gale Observatory. The data is processed with programming in PV-Wave . Below are the CCD image and the extracted HR diagram.

  • From Reflectivity Spectrum to Composite Lightcurve of the Asteroid.

    We study the classifications of asteroids based on their shapes and intensity of their reflectivity spectra. We compare the reflectivity spectra of various groups of asteroids and meteorites to see the connection in their evolution. We conclude that asteroids and meteorites of similar composition are likely tohave similar reflectivity spectra. We also estimate the rotational period of asteroid 125 Liberatrix from its light curve, which turn out to be very accurate for small celestial objects.

  • What is QCD ?
    In physics, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is the theory of the strong interaction between quarks and gluons, which are the fundamental particles that make up composite hadrons such as protons, neutrons, pions, etc, which eventually makes up our world.
