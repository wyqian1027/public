Data Analysis of Professional Starcraft 2.

  • What is Starcraft 2?

    If you are not familiar already, Starcraft 2 is a science fiction Real-Time Strategy(RTS) video game developed and published by Blizzard Entertainment in 2010, a sequel to its original 1998 video game StarCraft. Players can choose from three interstellar races: Terran, Zerg, and Protoss to battle against each other or form alliances. Diversified strategies can be used to win the game. Starcraft/Starcraft 2 is considered by many the most competitive professional game ever built, and its popularity and influences literally defined RTS at its prime.

  • Learning From Statistics - "A picture is worth a thousand words"

    Besides predictions, the data is helpful to answer some of the most interesting and concerned questions regarding game industry.

    Q: Is the distribution of player earnings from tournaments a normal Gaussian distribution?

    Actually no. The distribution is strong skewed. More than 50% of the Professional Starcraft 2 players are in the 0 ~ $100K tournament prize money range. The same observations are found in each race separately.

    Q: What about the distribution of players, by country?

    Any diehard Starcraft 2 fan probably knows this result, but it is still amazing to see with all the data. More than 64 % of the player are from South Korea. It is greater than the population of players combine else where. Most foreign players would come to South Korea to live and practise for months because the environment for Esports is just too good. Esports, especially Starcraft, is literally South Korea's national sports. It is rumored that one would need to beat his Korean fiancée's father in Starcraft in order to marry his daughter.

    Q: What is the relationship between player's age and his success in tournaments?

    From the plot we see negative correlations between the player's success (measured by tournament earnings) and the player's age, in each Starcraft race and as a whole. Since Starcraft 2 is a very fast-paced Action-Per-Minute (APM) intensive competitve game, younger players benefit a lot from faster reaction time than older players. So it is expected. However, the correlation is not very large. Detailed calculations showed the correlations to be -0.300, -0.281, -0.289 for Terran, Zerg and Protoss players . The overall correlation is around -0.286. This result indicates that game strategy is probably another crucial factor in the outcome of the matches.

  • Web Scrapper

    Python has very powerful libraries such as urllib.request and bs4.BeautifulSoup, which makes scrapping data straightforward. Following is an example. Once the "soup" (scrapped info) is obtained, one can parse the HTML page to extract useful information.

    In this application, a seed player is first selected and parsed (for example, "Maru" from JinAir Greenwings Club). Then, by using Breath-First Search (BFS) recursively on all his tournament opponents, match information and player information of all players from each premium tournament is scrapped successfully.

    Jupyter Notebook: Starcraft2LiquidPedia_Scrapper.ipynb

    Player csv file: playerInfo.csv
    Match Record csv file: playerMatchRecord.csv 
    from urllib.request import urlopen as uReq
from urllib.request import Request as Req
from bs4 import BeautifulSoup as soup

def makeSoup(url):
  req = Req(
      url, 
      data=None
  )
  try:
      uClient = uReq(req)
  except HTTPError:
      print("HTTPError...")
      return False
  except URLError:
      print("URLError...")
      return False
  except:
      print("Something bad happend...")
      return False
  else:
      pageHTML = uClient.read()
      uClient.close()
      return soup(pageHTML, "html.parser")
