Movie Rating Analysis of IMDB and DouBan

  • Movie Ratings on IMDB vs DouBan

    IMDB is one of the largest movie rating websites of the world, while DouBan is the dominate movie rating website in China. Below is a particular rating distribution for the 2009 movie title "Fantastic Mr. Fox"


  • Scrapping Methods

    The scrapper is based on Beautiful Python library. In practice, I selected 2-3 movie titles as the initial seed. The scrapper starts with the initial seed file as the Queue. Then the scrapper simulataneously crawled on the both websites and scraped the rating infos. Once each movie is finished, the scrapper add related movies in the recommendations to the Queue and move onto the next movie.

    Repeted movie titles are avoided. Delibrate timeout is used to prevent high traffic. All movie ratings are renormalized so both ratings used 1-10 rating system.

    With around 12 hours, we scrapped 4920 movie titles, and the distribution of genres are as follows.

  • Some Discoveries

    1. Top 10 Movies

    We found out that "The Shawshank's Redemption", "Schindler's List", " 12 Angry Men", "Forrest Gump" are the top 10 movies in both rating systems, making up 4 out of the 10 movies. It shows that audiences have pretty similar mindset in determining the best movies, despite the vast cultural differences between the West and China.


    2. Rating Distribution Patterns

    Here we show the total rating distribution pattern for all 4920 movies, as well as selected categories, including the leading category "Drama" with 2027 movie titles, and the underrepresented category "War" with 120 movie titles. In addition, rating for my favorite movie Fantastic Mr. Fox is also shown.

    Interestingly, all ratings follow a right tilted distribution, more like a Poisson or Gamma Distribution. DouBan and IMDB are in good agreement.


    3. Correlations of all Movie Scores.

    Lastly, we show the correlation of movie scores from both systems for all 4920 movies as below. It is clear indication that both ratings are comparable as they are strongly correlated, with a value of 0.788. People seem to share the same preferences on movies despite of their backgrounds. Notice the blob centered around 6-8 scores in the plot. It tells us that most of our movies are in this region. Compared with spread out region of 2-5 scores, it also seem to suggest that "good movies are usually agreed more by the audience". Further studies are needed.
